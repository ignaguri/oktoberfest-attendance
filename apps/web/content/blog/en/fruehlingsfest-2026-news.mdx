# Frühlingsfest 2026: Dates, Prices & What's New for the 60th Anniversary

Munich's beloved spring festival is celebrating a milestone this year. The **60th Frühlingsfest** returns to the Theresienwiese with a historic first: an extended run of three full weeks. Here's everything confirmed so far for the 2026 edition.

## Official Dates: April 17 - May 10, 2026

Frühlingsfest 2026 runs from **Friday, April 17 through Sunday, May 10** — three weeks instead of the usual two. The extension marks the festival's 60th anniversary, making this the longest Frühlingsfest in history.

May 1 (Labour Day) falls within the festival dates, giving visitors an extra public holiday to enjoy.

### Opening Hours

- **Monday - Friday**: 11:00 AM - 11:00 PM
- **Saturday, Sunday & May 1**: 10:00 AM - 11:00 PM
- **Last beer orders**: 10:30 PM

## Confirmed Beer Prices

Beer prices for 2026 have been confirmed, and they remain significantly cheaper than Oktoberfest:

| Tent | Brewery | Price per Maß |
|------|---------|---------------|
| **Festhalle Bayernland** | Augustiner | €13.50 |
| **Hippodrom** | Spaten | €14.50 |

For comparison, Oktoberfest 2025 prices ranged from €14.90 to €15.90 per Maß. Frühlingsfest continues to offer one of the best value-for-money beer festival experiences in Munich.

## The Two Beer Tents

### Festhalle Bayernland

The oldest tent at Frühlingsfest serves **Augustiner** beer — widely regarded as Munich's finest brewery. Known for its traditional Bavarian atmosphere, the Festhalle Bayernland features live brass bands and Bavarian music daily. Augustiner's famous wooden-barrel beer (Holzfassbier) is a highlight that purists travel specifically for.

Reservations are available online at festhalle-bayernland.de for tables of 10 or more.

### Hippodrom

Run by the Krätz family, the **Hippodrom** serves **Spaten** beer and seats approximately 2,000 guests. It's known for its dual personality: business-friendly during the day with a livelier party atmosphere in the evenings. The tent features live bands and brass ensembles daily.

Reservations are available online at hippodrom.de for tables of 10 or more.

Both tents offer **free entry** — no tickets required. Walk-ins are possible, especially on weekdays.

## What's New for the 60th Anniversary

### Three Weeks for the First Time

The biggest change for 2026 is the extended duration. The festival typically runs for about two weeks, but the 60th anniversary has earned it a full three-week run from April 17 to May 10. This means more opportunities to visit, less pressure to plan around a narrow window, and a more relaxed atmosphere overall.

### Rides and Attractions

The 2026 edition features over **100 vendors** with more than **30 rides**, including:

- A **50+ meter Willenborg Ferris wheel** — the largest ever at Frühlingsfest
- Classic thrill rides, bumper cars, and family-friendly attractions
- Food stalls spanning traditional Bavarian fare to festival snacks

### Special Events

- **Fireworks**: Scheduled for Friday evenings during the festival
- **Family Day**: Tuesdays from 12:00 to 7:00 PM with discounted ride prices and family-friendly programming
- **Bavarian Red Cross Flea Market**: First Saturday of the festival
- **Classic Car Meeting**: Held on one of the Tuesdays
- **Day of Customs (Tag der Trachten)**: Celebrating Bavarian tradition

## Getting There

The festival is held on the northern section of the **Theresienwiese** — the same grounds as Oktoberfest, but a smaller footprint.

- **U-Bahn**: U4/U5 to **Theresienwiese** (recommended)
- **S-Bahn**: Hackerbrücke, then a 10-minute walk
- **On foot**: 15 minutes from Munich Hauptbahnhof

Driving is discouraged — there's no parking on or near the grounds.
